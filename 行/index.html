going

Reading:

On-Yomi: コウ、ギョウ、アン — Kun-Yomi: い.く、ゆ.く、-ゆ.き、-ゆき、-い.き、-いき、おこな.う、おこ.なう

Heisig story:

By joining the top four strokes, you should get a picture of the front current of a river, the stream trailing behind. Hence the character for going.

Heisig comment:

* As a primitive, this character has two forms. Reduced to the left side only, it can mean a column, going, or a line of something or other. When the middle is opened up to make room for other elements, it means a boulevard.
