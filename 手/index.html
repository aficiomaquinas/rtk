hand

Reading:

On-Yomi: シュ、ズ — Kun-Yomi: て、て-、-て、た-

Heisig story:

Any way you count them, there are either too many or too few fingers to see a good pictograph of a hand in this character. But that it is, and so you must.

Heisig comment:

* Keep to the etymology when using this kanji as a primitive: a single hand all by itself.

Koohii stories:

1) [decamer0n] 31-1-2007(99): This pictograph actually really works if you look at it as a hand with fingers spread and middle finger pointing down. see the very top stroke as the edge of a shirt cuff, and it will all come together.

2) [mantixen] 4-8-2009(86): A hand is useful for 千一 (1001) things.

3) [dingomick] 25-2-2007(33): Hello. My name is Inigo Montoya. You killed my father. Prepare to die. (the 6-fingered man).

4) [smithsonian] 23-11-2006(13): Heisig says it's not a very good pictograph of a human hand, but it is a good pictograph of a cartoon hand. Picture Mickey Mouse's distinctive white glove with its three fingers and a thumb. The kanji that use this element can also use Mickey Mouse as a primitive element in place of hand.

5) [Tornadic_Indignation] 4-5-2009(7): The slightly bent nail went through two of the hand's fingers (this one is pretty easy to memorize).
