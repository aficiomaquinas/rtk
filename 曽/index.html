formerly

Reading:

On-Yomi: ソウ、ソ、ゾウ — Kun-Yomi: かつ、かつて、すなわち

Heisig story:

This primitive (named for its associations with the kanji of the following frame) is composed of a pair of horns growing out of a brain with a tongue wagging in the mouth beneath. Think of "former" in connection with administrators or heads of state who have just left office but continue to make a nuisance of themselves by advertising their opinions on public policy.

Heisig comment:

* The primitive meaning, increase, comes from the next frame. Always think of something multiplying wildly as you watch.
