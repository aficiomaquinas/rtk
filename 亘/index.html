span

Reading:

On-Yomi: コウ、カン — Kun-Yomi: わた.る、もと.める

Heisig story:

"Sunrise, sunset, sunrise, sunset..." goes the song of the Fiddler on the Roof. You can almost see the journey of the sun as it moves from one horizon (the floor) to its noonday heights in the sky overhead (ceiling) and then disappears over the other horizon - day after day, marking the span of our lives.

Koohii stories:

1) [Christine_Tham] 21-7-2007(227): The sun spans from horizon to horizon in a day.

2) [nihonnicko] 1-5-2008(45): The span of the day is the time between the sun goes up and comes down.

3) [ashman63] 2-2-2008(17): The sunlight spans from the floor to the ceiling.

4) [rakutenka] 30-6-2007(9): The sun spans the heaven and the earth.

5) [kanpai] 27-1-2011(8): A sun span-wich.
