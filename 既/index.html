previously

Reading:

On-Yomi: キ — Kun-Yomi: すで.に

Primitive:

waitress If you draw this character once, you will see that its first three strokes resemble the form for receipt (except that the second stroke ends more parallel to the first), with its last stroke stretched to form the first of the two human legs. From this we give it its meaning of a waitress (who should not be confused with the waiter back in FRAME 976 侍). [4]

Heisig story:

Silver . . . waitress. Do not confuse this kanji's key word with before (Frame 263).
