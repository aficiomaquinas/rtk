current

Reading:

On-Yomi: リュウ、ル — Kun-Yomi: なが.れる、なが.れ、なが.す、-なが.す

Heisig story:

Water . . . infant . . . flood. Be sure to distinguish the two water primitives from one another in making your story.

Koohii stories:

1) [Dragg] 21-11-2007(254): INFANT Moses placed in the WATER and sent down the current of the STREAM.

2) [fuaburisu] 10-10-2005(57): A water-breathing infant (genetically modified?) is caught in a flood, and drifts with the current, apparently enjoying himself in the process.

3) [imojyochu] 15-8-2008(29): An INFANT fell in the WATER and was lost down the CURRENT of the STREAM.

4) [numirabis] 1-3-2009(19): The best way to deal with a stream of infant doodie is watery current.

5) [sgrant] 31-10-2008(18): …(continued from previous kanji). Unfortunately, the water-level rose and the poor infant was washed away by the stream's current… dying a sulphurous death.
