orderliness

Reading:

On-Yomi: カイ — Kun-Yomi: かな.う、 やわ.らぐ

Heisig story:

The harmoney of "a place for everything and everything in its place" is applied here to the orderliness of all the words one speaks.

Koohii stories:

1) [thecite] 11-1-2010(38): harmonious: If a choir is to be harmonious, all the voices need to combine to hit the right notes, and say the right words.

2) [perrin4869] 14-9-2010(14): Think of a chat in which everyone is writing words at once, of the lack of orderliness of such a chat.

3) [chameleoncoder] 19-8-2010(11): To have orderliness, your words must be in all the right places.

4) [chibimizuno] 1-6-2011(8): 起立 (きりつ)！ Hearing these words, all the students immediately stand at attention. The orderliness of classes in Japan is incredible.

5) [Elphalpo] 5-7-2010(8): to be in sync: The words a choir sings must all be in sync. ~~~ (Note: According to Yahoo! 辞書 , this character means something like "to harmonize/to mitigate."
