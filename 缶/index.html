tin can

Reading:

On-Yomi: カン — Kun-Yomi: かま

Heisig story:

Though the meaning has no reference to animals, the parts do: horse with a mountain underneath.

Koohii stories:

1) [Piitaa] 13-7-2006(245): Horses kept in the mountains have a unique savory taste. At least so the text on this tin can would have us believe, which contains 100% authentic mountain horse.

2) [Zactacular] 8-10-2007(62): The starving horse, stranded on the mountain, is forced to eat a tasty tin can.

3) [Perry] 1-5-2010(33): Hint: pictograph of a horse standing in a tin can. No need for mountain.

4) [ezmoreldo] 3-7-2008(32): Mountains can be pretty rough on horses' hooves so it might help to put tin cans on each hoof as an extra horseshoe.

5) [Mary_K] 19-6-2009(18): By noon, the mountain is covered with tin cans.
