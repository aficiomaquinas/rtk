On-Yomi: ヤク

Heisig story:

Consider for a moment the etymology of the word "promise" in order to notice its roots in the activity of putting one thing (e.g., one's word of honor) in place of another (e.g., the fulfillment of a task). For as it turns out, this character also means "to abridge, economize, and abbreviate" - all activities that involve putting one thing in place of another. With that in mind, we may now work with the elements: thread . . . ladle.
