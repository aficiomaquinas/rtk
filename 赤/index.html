red

Reading:

On-Yomi: セキ、シャク — Kun-Yomi: あか、あか-、あか.い、あか.らむ、あか.らめる

Heisig story:

Ground . . . dagger . . . little. The two strokes of the dagger take the place of the middle stroke of little.

Heisig comment:

* As a primitive on the left, this kanji keeps the same form. Elsewhere, the first two strokes are abbreviated to a single dot. This latter form will take the meaning of an apple.

Koohii stories:

1) [Copycatken] 30-6-2006(204): He was stabbed with a dagger, and little drops of red blood dropped onto the ground.

2) [Django] 30-9-2007(55): There was red all over the soil after Little Red Riding Hood plunged a dagger into the wolf. Those two dots we see are wolfies animal legs we see, by the way (a la smithsonian).

3) [smithsonian] 25-1-2007(24): (I prefer to use animal legs instead of little so as not to resort to combining it with dagger:) The cow refused to leave the temple grounds, even when it was threatened with a dagger. Its animal legs just stayed planted firmly there so it could show its devotion to Buddha. This is the story of ''akabeko'', the red cow, a popular myth in the Aizu region of Japan.

4) [jmcanally] 10-2-2010(13): This is a mass grave for animal and human legs. The serial killer is a Japanese guy with short legs and long-leg envy, so he cuts off legs that are longer than his and buries them here. Notice that since the legs have been cut off one-by-one and tossed in the grave randomly so the animal legs look a bit far apart and the human legs look a bit different than usual (direction of the curve of the right leg is opposite the usual). The ground is still RED with from all the blood spilled.

5) [koohiikun] 15-10-2009(12): The soil was red after the hunter used his dagger to cut off the animal's legs.
