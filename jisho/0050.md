## Heisig story:

Here we see our famous miraculous <i>magic wand</i> hanging, all on its own, <b>below</b> the <i>ceiling</i>, as you probably already guessed would happen. In addition to giving us two new kanji, the two shapes given in this and the preceding frame also serve to fix the use of the primitives for <i>ceiling</i> and <i>floor</i>, by drawing our attention successively to the line standing above and <b>below</b> the primitive element to which it is related.
