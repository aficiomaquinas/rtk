winding

Reading:

On-Yomi: ソウ — Kun-Yomi: く.る

Heisig story:

Thread . . . goods . . . tree.

Koohii stories:

1) [PacifistGod] 2-8-2007(155): After stopping a bank heist, Spiderman is seen winding his webbing around the stolen goods to trap them in a tree until the police arrive to take them back.

2) [Katsuo] 28-2-2008(116): Tip: goods + wood/tree : This combination appears 4 times. I suggest calling it "furniture" (a common type of wooden goods). REF: parch (#215 燥) , maneuver (#671 操), winding (FRAME 1369), seaweed (#2035 藻). Story: You are winding up your new furniture using a thread to get it through the window of your high-rise apartment.

3) [shaydwyrm] 13-5-2008(81): Spiderman often accidentally breaks his furniture (thanks Katsuo!) with his spider strength, so he is often seen winding his webbing around various pieces to repair them.

4) [herman] 28-11-2009(41): Primary meaning of this kanji has to do with turning pages/leafing through a book so I focus on that meaning. Also taking the furniture suggestion from Katsuo about the primitive on the right and making it even more concrete by having it refer to IKEA. Here I see Spiderman reading an IKEA catalogue, he's lost in thought leafing through the pages.

5) [CountPacula] 14-7-2008(21): Winding thread around wooden goods to ready them for safe transport.
