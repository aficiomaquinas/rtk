## Koohii stories: 

1) [<a href="http://kanji.koohii.com/profile/CharleyGarrett">CharleyGarrett</a>] 2-12-2006(41): <strong>Ingest</strong> is a fancy word for what you do with your <em>mouth</em> as you <em>eat</em>.

2) [<a href="http://kanji.koohii.com/profile/Katsuo">Katsuo</a>] 6-10-2007(19): Note: This is a colloquial word for &quot;eat&quot;, so the formal keyword &quot;ingest&quot; might better be replaced with something like &quot;scoff&quot; or &quot;tuck in&quot;, etc. Suggested story: &quot;Yeuk! I can see the <em>food</em> rolling around in your <em>mouth</em> when you <strong>scoff</strong> it like that!&quot;.

3) [<a href="http://kanji.koohii.com/profile/billyclyde">billyclyde</a>] 21-5-2008(5): It&#039;s too simple! MOUTH &amp; FOOD eatquals--<strong> ingest</strong>! You<strong> ingest</strong> so much food you<strong> ingest</strong> your own mouth. Slow down, champ.

4) [<a href="http://kanji.koohii.com/profile/Meconium">Meconium</a>] 10-4-2011(2): <strong>Ingest</strong> implies something that goes in your <em>mouth</em> and you swallow it, not necessarily something you deliberately <em>eat</em>. If you stick your head out the window of a car and <strong>ingest</strong> a fly, it&#039;s not the same as deliberately <em>eat</em>ing a fly at the dinner table. [Note: what Katsuo says is true; this kanji means <strong>OM NOM NOM NOM</strong>.
